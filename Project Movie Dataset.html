Introduction¶

In this movie dataset more than 10.000 movies and their features are listed. Some of the features are Genre, Revenue, Year or Director. Over the course of the analysis of this movie dataset I want to answer the following questions:

  • What's the mean adjusted budget and revenue over the years (1960 - 2015)?
  • What's the most popular genre over the years?
  • Who are the top 5 directors in terms of adjusted revenue earned with their respective movies?
  • Do more popular movies get better scores?

Conclusions¶

That concludes my analysis of this dataset. What can be said about the dataset? If a movie has a "high" popularity level it leads by average to a better score than scores from all the other popularity levels. The director with most revenue made is Steven Spielberg (around 15 billion dollar) and the most popular genres over the last 40 years are either Drama or Comedy.

Limitations¶

It has to be said that many missing values in form of "0" values for budget and revenue make it harder to analyse those features. Means for budgets and revenues columns are skewed because of the many 0 values. Further analysis regarding budget and revenue has to be made. Why are so few movies from earlier days in the set? Do we not have enough info about them? Maybe it would be better that analyse years that have a minimum of movies or a minimum of "0" values. Additionally it would be best to get have something like a "popularity_adj" column because popularity can be better tracked over the years with social media rising since the early 2000s. Can we account for that before social media?
